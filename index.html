"Climathon is a global 24-hour climate change hackathon which will take place simultaneously in major cities around the world on 27 October 2017. It brings together the challenges of the world’s cities with the people who have the passion and ability to solve them. Climathon attracts innovators, entrepreneurs, students and professionals to create innovative solutions to cities climate challenges.[1] For Karlsruhe's climathon, we are seeking to start solutions, which are profitable within 5 years or are of compelling social value to warrant social investment and action. Karlsruhe is a young city with a deep climate science and entreprenuerial culture. As well as being a signatory for the Global Covenant of Mayors, the city hosts a regional climate research center at KIT-Nord, ... [other instutitions here]. On its own, the city offers multiple programs such as... If you look beyond the local science community, you will find an entreprenuerial network that provides [services] from [entrepreurial agencies]. This climathon leverages these assets for hackathon solutions that help make them a reality. To selected projects, we help provide guidance and support to move efforts to the next level. Since this effort originates from the UNFCCC, access to these networks as well as the potential to participate in KIC-Climate Entreprenuerial Challenges is highly possible when developed solutions can benefit cities other than Karlsruhe.

Program for the Climathon

  • Friday - October 27, 2017
  • Knowledge Workshops of How Climate Change Affects Karlsruhe
  • Local Leaders Discuss Local Impact: Design Challenges
  • Skills and Tech Workshops by Participants
  • Open Space Discussion and Incubation
  • Teams Form: Brainstorm
  • Idea Hacking and Prototyping
  • Saturday - October 28, 2017
  • Business Model Canvas
  • Team Prototype Gallery
  • Stakeholder Review and Voting
  • Winner or Accepted Projects Selected and Action Planning
  • The program is subject to revision or changes.

Event Location: Future Room by Quartier Zukunft

Karlsruhe is unique in its capacity to address the localized effects of climate change through its efforts in Sustainable Development. Quartier Zukunft has created a space where scientist can work with local citizens to study how different theories and methods can improve the local community in Oststadt. Their work is important in a world where scientists and academics are not viewed as a part of their local communities. These scientists are.
